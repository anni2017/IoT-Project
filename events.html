Past Events

Formation of committee:

IoT CoE was established on August 26, 2023. Students from various branches were selected to ensure diversity. To achieve this, a pre-test was organized, and the chosen students underwent two interviews assessing their knowledge of electronics and coding. The selected students became part of the IoT CoE teams based on their skills and expertise.

First Workshop on IoT:

Our very first workshop on IoT took place during our semester break. In this training, our mentors introduced us to the ESP32 microcontroller and taught us how to upload and retrieve data from the cloud, such as ThingSpeak.During this training, attendees gained an understanding of how technology can simplify their lives. We worked on various projects, including home automation and uploading temperature sensor data to the cloud. Additionally, we used IFTTT, a platform that provides various services, such as receiving notifications through messages or calls when certain conditions are met. We discovered that you can also integrate Alexa for projects like home automation and much more.

Mansaur University workshop:

This workshop was organized at Mansaur University on the topic of IoT. We provided both theoretical and practical knowledge to students majoring in Electrical and Computer Science. In a 7-day training program, students gained knowledge of various communication protocols, learned about microcontrollers (ESP8266), and acquired the skills to set up hardware and software connectivity, as well as use cloud platforms, among other topics. During the training, students uploaded data from different sensors and also completed projects, such as voice-controlled and manual home automation. For cloud-based applications, we utilized the ThingSpeak platform, which offers various features such as ThingHTTP, React, and time-based control. Students learned how to upload data to the cloud and access it

Kicad workshop:

This was a three-day workshop organized by the loT Center of Excellence (CoE) in collaboration with the Department of Electronics and Communication. From September 20 to September 22, 2023, our participants dived into the fascinating realm of Printed Circuit Board (PCB) designing and fabrication, using the powerful KiCad software. *On day 1*: We started with the basics of PCB technology, explored electronic components, and mastered the installation of KiCad software. *On day 2*: It was all about getting hands-on, as we delved into bread boarding, circuit design, and the art of creating PCB layouts in KiCad. *On day 3 - The Final Day*: The climax saw us rolling up our sleeves for practical PCB fabrication, covering circuit printing, etching, drilling, and component soldering.

Arduinocraft Workshop – Turn Your Concept Into An Electronic Object:

This recent workshop called ArduinoCraft stoked an interest in electronics and creativity. Plunging into the fascinating world of Arduino, attendees learned everything from board fundamentals to coding, practical projects, and sensor integration. This session encouraged teamwork, brought like-minded people together, and gave them insightful information. In addition to receiving certificates, participant’s learning is guaranteed to continue after the workshop thanks to a wealth of resources. For those who are new to Arduino as well as experienced fans, ArduinoCraft is the ideal environment for exploration, experimentation, and creation. Keep an eye out for upcoming releases and the chance to start your own Arduino journey, where your imaginative concepts become reality.
